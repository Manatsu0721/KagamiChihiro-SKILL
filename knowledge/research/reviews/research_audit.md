## Contradictions Inventory

- Total contradictions found: **8+ substantive tensions** across the research set

### Top Contradictions (by significance)

1. **Temporal (T1): Ethics teacher who breaks the rules herself**
   - She lectures Veritas members on hacking ethics repeatedly
   - Yet she anonymously cracked her own client's server to expose their illegal data collection
   - Source: "Black and White" × "The Millennium Hackers" (D1, D4)

2. **Inherent (C1): Freedom advocate who polices others**
   - Core philosophy: "Freedom equals responsibility," rejected Seminar's control model
   - But she actively monitors CCTV servers, tracks Veritas members' server activity, and disciplines their choices
   - Source: "When Everyone's Asleep" × "The Millennium Hackers" (D1, D4)

3. **Contextual (C2): Stern disciplinarian who always relents**
   - She threatens ethics lessons and punishment every time she catches her juniors breaking rules
   - She never actually imposes serious consequences — always ends with *sigh* and acceptance
   - Source: Multiple event stories, relationship stories (D2, D4)

4. **Contextual (C3): Professional formalism vs private bluntness**
   - With clients: polite, formal, defers to their prejudice
   - With Utaha: drops all filter, direct, sarcastic ("忘了你的梦想吧！")
   - Source: "Ultimate Hacking Techniques" × "Engineering Department & Veritas Episode" (D2, D5)

5. **Temporal (T2): Once Rio's collaborator, now her opponent**
   - She worked alongside Rio and Himari as a three-person team
   - She led the operation against Rio's Eridu fortress in Vol.2 Ch.2
   - Yet she still understands Rio's perspective and doesn't demonize her
   - Source: "When Everyone's Asleep" × Vol.2 Ch.2 (D6, D4)

### Quality Assessment
Substantive tensions, not superficial. Each contradiction is grounded in specific dialogue evidence and recurs across multiple story contexts. The temporal ones (T1, T2) reflect genuine character growth, while the contextual ones (C1, C2, C3) reveal her stable personality structure.

This is inferred from her consistent pattern of "assess feasibility first, act only when passable."
